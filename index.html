Eli Schiff is a design systems lead and consultant, design critic and podcast host.
‍
In his design practice, Eli spearheads design systems efforts for both startups and enterprise. Projects have included work with IBM, MIT, TEDX, CDC, Internet Association and Design Museum, among others.
‍
Eli's research and writing focuses on interface design history, theory and culture from a formalist perspective. He is the author of Uber's Undoing, a book about Uber's design history from 2010 until 2018. He is the host of the In Depth podast. He also runs the Humanist Interface newsletter.
‍
Eli's writing has appeared in publications including Offscreen, Washington Post, Subtraction, Recode, CNBC, O'Reilly, BGR, Daring Fireball, Time, Frieze, The Outline, Fortune and more.
